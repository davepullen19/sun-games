# MYSTERY KEEP
## The Daily Deduction Quest

A modern daily riddle game built around contradiction, implication, and hidden logic.

Inspired by:
- classic fantasy board game box art
- detective fiction
- daily ritual games like Wordle
- “spot the impossible” internet culture

---

# Core Concept

Players are presented with:
- a short scenario
- several statements
- a contradiction hidden in the details

Their task is to:
## identify the critical inconsistency

This is NOT a traditional joke-riddle game.

The experience should feel like:
- decoding a hidden system
- detecting impossible logic
- uncovering lies
- understanding how the world works

Over time, players become fluent in the game’s “language.”

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

## Each Daily Chamber Contains:
- 1 short narrative scene
- 3–5 statements
- 1 hidden contradiction
- 4 possible answers

Example:

> The royal baker swore nobody entered the kitchen overnight.
>
> At dawn, the oven was still warm.
>
> Snow covered the courtyard outside every door and window.
>
> The guard captain's boots were completely dry.

Question:
## Which detail proves someone is lying?

Choices:
- THE OVEN
- THE BOOTS
- THE SNOW
- THE WINDOW

Answer:
## THE BOOTS

Reason:
If snow covered every entrance, anyone entering the castle would have wet footwear.

---

# Design Philosophy

The challenge should come from:
- implication
- logic
- contradiction
- precision
- deduction

NOT:
- obscure trivia
- impossible leaps
- random riddles
- trick answers

The ideal player reaction is:
## “OH.”

That instant realization is the emotional payoff.

---

# Core Pillars

## 1. Fast
A puzzle should take:
- 30–90 seconds

This creates habitual play.

---

## 2. Learnable
Players gradually learn:
- physical logic
- social logic
- semantic logic
- causal logic

The game develops mastery over time.

---

## 3. Shareable
Players should want to post:
- streaks
- solve count
- difficulty
- “I got Chamber 14 in 1”

---

## 4. Elegant
Every puzzle should have:
- one clean solution
- a satisfying explanation
- minimal ambiguity

# Difficulty Structure

## Easy
Physical contradictions.

Example:
A man standing in rain is completely dry.

---

## Medium
Social contradictions.

Example:
A woman claims she never met someone but later references their habits.

---

## Hard
Semantic contradictions.

Example:
Statements technically true but logically incompatible.

---

## Expert
Assumption traps.

The player must detect:
- hidden implications
- framing tricks
- perspective shifts

# Why This Has Potential

Most daily games are:
- vocabulary tests
- trivia
- reaction games

Mystery Keep becomes:
## a daily intelligence ritual

It trains:
- observation
- deduction
- logical consistency
- implication detection

And because it is text-only:
- cheap to produce
- infinitely scalable
- mobile friendly
- highly shareable
- easy to localize
